Stability
In order to safeguard the sustainability of this metals-based energy supply chain it is a
commercial imperative that Integrated Energy Materials be defined by its engagement as a
responsible stakeholder within the communities of which we are a part.

The Company has three key guiding principles:

Transparency:
  • Tracking all energy materials through the supply chain to ensure known material content and cleanliness. We will make this data accessible to retail end users to other constituents, including NGOs.
  • Being able to trace clean content is a key part to 21st century conscientious consumption. Institutionalizing this standard is key to scaling.
Economic equity:
  • Bring greater prosperity to the communities endowed by the natural resources of energy metals.
  • Expand the labor base and higher-wage jobs through onshoring beneficiation and ensuring that the local workforce is fully represented.
  • Pay taxes in the jurisdiction where the economic activity occurs, not in low-tax offshore jurisdictions.
Social inclusion:
  • IEM is only as strong as the members of its community. We will work with, including providing financing for, world-class partners to ensure the provision of education, healthcare, affordable housing, and clean water.
